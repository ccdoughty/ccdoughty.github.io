The environments and hosts of metal absorption at z > 5 (arXiv:2211.07313)

Using the cosmological radiation-hydrodynamic simulation Technicolor Dawn, I studied the association between metals and galaxies at z>5. Technicolor is a unique simulation in that it self-consistently models many frequencies for the radiative transfer, more faithfully capturing the number of photons at ionization energies for many metal transitions. We focused on several transitions of high interest (OI, MgII, SiII, CII, SiIV, and CIV). The main findings are summarized below, supported by a few of the figures from the work.

  • Stronger metal absorption is associated with higher overdensities and higher SFR. However, the correlation is stronger with overdensity. There is an anticorrelation between strength and impact parameter. We also find that the vast majority of metal systems occur outside the virial radius of the nearest galaxy.

  • Prior to the end of reionization, the cross-correlation between galaxies and high ionization metals is slightly greater than that between galaxies and low ionization metals. Post-reionization, low reionization metals are more strongly associated with galaxies.


In the future, it would be interesting to compare to metal absorption observations from EIGER to better understand the association between metals and galaxies at high z.
